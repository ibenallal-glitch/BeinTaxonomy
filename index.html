A
Global Taxonomy

Global Taxonomy +
Country Layer

One universal content tree (Sport → Competition → Team/Player) with a country/region filter overlay applied at query time. The datalayer emits the same keys across all markets; segmentation happens downstream.

⚡ Best for: Global Analytics & BI Dashboards
✓ Pros
  • Single source of truth for all markets
  • Cross-market performance benchmarking
  • Centralised data governance & taxonomy
  • Simpler datalayer schema to maintain
  • Easier global reporting in tools like GA4, BigQuery
✗ Cons
  • Less granular for local editorial teams
  • Country-specific sports can feel secondary
  • Push notification targeting needs extra logic
  • Higher risk of misclassification at local level
✓ SELECTED
B
Country-First

Country-First
Segmentation

The taxonomy root starts with region/country cluster (MENA, Europe, APAC, Americas, Turkey), then branches into the sports and competitions that matter most in that specific market.

⚡ Best for: Personalisation & Push Notifications
✓ Pros
  • Native fit for personalisation engines & CDPs
  • Reflects actual user interest by market
  • Enables hyper-local editorial curation
  • location_language already in the datalayer
  • Better for targeted push & notification flows
✗ Cons
  • Harder to compare cross-market metrics
  • Taxonomy duplication (UCL appears in every region)
  • Higher maintenance overhead as markets expand
  • Risk of data silos between regions

Data & Analytics Use Cases

→ Use Proposal A

Global taxonomy ensures consistent measurement across all 43+ markets. Essential for comparing UEFA Champions League viewership between MENA and France, or tracking cross-market content performance in BigQuery / GA4.

  • 📊BI dashboards & executive reporting across markets
  • 🔬A/B testing content strategies globally
  • 📈Content performance benchmarking (UCL in MENA vs EU)
  • 🏛️Centralised data governance & taxonomy management
  • 🔗Attribution modelling from content → subscription
  • 💾Datalayer key: content_competition same value everywhere

Personalisation & UX Use Cases

→ Use Proposal B

Country-first segmentation enables hyper-local personalisation. A user in Qatar sees a Süper Lig notification only when a Turkish club is involved in a competition they follow — not because Süper Lig ranks globally.

  • 🎯Targeted push notifications by market sport priority
  • 🏠Homepage content curation for local editorial teams
  • 🤖ML personalisation models (CDPs, recommendation engines)
  • ⭐favorite_teams & favorite_competitions UX flows
  • 📱App onboarding sport selection by location_language
  • 🔔Datalayer key: location_language drives segmentation
